About Me

Third-year Ph.D. student specializing in comparative politics and international security

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in Political Science and a predoctoral fellow at the Violence, Conflict, and Security Lab at the University of Florida. I also hold an M.A. in International Affairs from Missouri State University and a B.A. in International Studies from the American University of Iraq, Sulaimani.

My research focuses on understanding the dynamics of political violence in divided societies. I employ both quantitative and qualitative methods to examine patterns of conflict and their underlying causes.

Dissertation In Progress

Subnational Variation in Communal Violence

PhD Dissertation ‚Ä¢ University of Florida

This research investigates why communal violence emerges and escalates in some localities but not others within divided societies. Using a mixed-methods approach combining quantitative analysis of conflict data with detailed case studies from the Middle East, I examine the local-level factors that enable or constrain violent mobilization across ethnic and sectarian lines.
